Description

The mango tree (Mangifera indica) is an evergreen fruit tree that grows best in warm, tropical and subtropical climates. It has a dense, rounded canopy and leathery dark-green leaves, and produces sweet, aromatic fruits rich in vitamins A and C. Mango trees perform best in deep, well-drained loamy soil and do not tolerate frost or prolonged cold conditions.

Care Information

Ideal Growing Conditions

  • Climate: Tropical and subtropical; frost free.
  • Temperature: 24–30 °C for best growth and fruiting.
  • Sunlight: Full sun with at least 6–8 hours of direct light daily.
  • Soil: Deep, well-drained loamy soil with pH 5.5–7.5.

Water Requirement

  • Young plants: water regularly every 3–4 days in summer to keep soil slightly moist.
  • Mature trees: moderate watering; can tolerate short dry periods.
  • Avoid overwatering and waterlogging to prevent root rot.
  • Increase irrigation during flowering and fruit development, then reduce before harvest for better fruit quality.

Common Diseases

  • Powdery mildew: White powdery growth on flowers and leaves; reduces fruit set.
  • Anthracnose: Black spots on leaves and fruits, especially in humid weather.
  • Mango malformation: Abnormally formed flower clusters and poor fruiting.

Pests

  • Mango hopper: Sucks sap from tender leaves and flowers.
  • Fruit fly: Lays eggs in ripening fruits, causing internal damage and rot.
  • Mealybugs: Produce sticky honeydew that can lead to sooty mold on leaves and stems.

Symptoms of Poor Health

  • Yellowing or spotted leaves.
  • Premature flower or fruit drop.
  • Fruit rotting or black patches on the skin.
  • Wilting, slow growth, or weak canopy development.

Preventive Care

  • Prune dead, diseased, and crowded branches annually to improve light and air circulation.
  • Maintain orchard sanitation by removing fallen leaves, diseased twigs, and dropped fruits.
  • Apply recommended fungicides during flowering season in areas prone to disease.
  • Ensure proper spacing between trees to reduce humidity and disease spread.
  • Use neem oil or other eco-friendly sprays early in the season for basic pest management.

Fertilizer Recommendation

  • Apply well-decomposed farmyard manure or compost around the tree every year.
  • Use a balanced NPK fertilizer (10:10:10 or 12:12:12) according to the tree’s age and size.
  • Split nitrogen applications: once before flowering and again after fruit set.
  • Where needed, supply micronutrients such as zinc and boron to improve fruit set and quality.
