Description

The Gulmohar tree (Delonix regia), also known as the Flame Tree, is a deciduous ornamental flowering tree prized for its bright red‑orange blossoms and broad, umbrella‑shaped canopy. It thrives in warm tropical climates and is widely planted along avenues, roadsides, campuses, and parks.

Care Information

Ideal Growing Conditions

  • Plant type: Deciduous ornamental flowering tree.
  • Climate: Tropical; sensitive to frost.
  • Temperature: 20–35 °C for best growth and flowering.
  • Sunlight: Full sun is required for profuse flowering.
  • Soil: Well-drained sandy or loamy soil, slightly acidic to neutral.

Water Requirement

  • Young plants: require regular watering until well established.
  • Mature trees: drought tolerant; need only occasional deep watering.
  • Avoid overwatering and waterlogged conditions to prevent root problems.
  • Reduce watering during monsoon if natural rainfall is sufficient.

Common Diseases

  • Root rot: Occurs mainly in poorly drained or waterlogged soils.
  • Leaf spot: Brown or black spots on leaves that may lead to premature leaf drop.
  • Powdery mildew: White fungal coating on leaves in humid weather.

Pests

  • Caterpillars: Feed on leaves and reduce foliage.
  • Aphids: Cause leaf curling and secrete honeydew, which can lead to sooty mold.
  • Scale insects: Suck sap and weaken branches and twigs.

Symptoms of Poor Health

  • Yellowing leaves and pale foliage.
  • Reduced or poor flowering compared to normal seasons.
  • Leaf drop occurring earlier than usual.
  • Stunted growth or sparse canopy.

Preventive Care

  • Ensure excellent soil drainage to avoid standing water and root rot.
  • Prune lightly to maintain shape, remove weak or crossing branches, and improve airflow.
  • Avoid excessive nitrogen fertilizer, which can increase leafy growth but reduce flowering.
  • Promptly remove and destroy infected leaves or branches to limit disease spread.
  • Use neem-based or similar eco-friendly sprays for early pest control.

Fertilizer Recommendation

  • Apply a balanced NPK fertilizer once or twice a year, especially during active growth.
  • Use organic manure or compost during early growth stages to build soil fertility.
  • Mature trees usually need minimal fertilization but may benefit from micronutrients in poor soils.
